# Parkinson's & movement disorders

> Dopamine in vivo. The cleanest molecular-imaging story in neurology — and a rapidly growing menu of MR biomarkers chasing the same biology.

Parkinson's disease (PD) and the related atypical parkinsonian syndromes converge on dysfunction of the nigrostriatal dopaminergic system. Unlike most neurodegenerative diseases, PD has a *strong* molecular biomarker (dopamine-transporter SPECT) that has been clinically deployed for two decades. Around it has grown a more recent constellation of MR biomarkers — neuromelanin-sensitive sequences, QSM-derived nigral iron, nigrosome-1 imaging — that are now changing what counts as "imaging-positive" PD.

For background on basal-ganglia neuroanatomy, dopaminergic systems, and the broader movement-disorder vocabulary, see [Fundamentals → Neuroscience & neurology](../fundamentals/foundations/neuroscience.md).

## Clinical picture

The cardinal motor features (the **TRAP** mnemonic):

- **T**remor — classically resting, 4–6 Hz, asymmetric onset.
- **R**igidity — increased tone, cogwheeling.
- **A**kinesia / bradykinesia — slowness and decrement of repetitive movement.
- **P**ostural instability — late feature; falls are a red flag for atypical parkinsonism.

Non-motor features matter for staging and differential: REM sleep behaviour disorder (RBD), hyposmia, constipation, orthostatic hypotension, depression, eventual cognitive decline / PD-dementia.

### Differential — PD vs atypical vs essential tremor

| Diagnosis | Pathology | Imaging giveaway |
|---|---|---|
| **Parkinson's disease (PD)** | α-synuclein (Lewy bodies) | DAT-SPECT deficit; SN nigrosome-1 loss; SN neuromelanin loss |
| **Progressive supranuclear palsy (PSP)** | 4R tauopathy | Midbrain "hummingbird sign"; SCP atrophy |
| **Multiple system atrophy (MSA)** | α-synuclein (glial) | Pontine "hot-cross-bun"; cerebellar atrophy (MSA-C); putaminal slit (MSA-P) |
| **Corticobasal degeneration (CBD)** | 4R tauopathy | Asymmetric perirolandic atrophy |
| **Dementia with Lewy bodies (DLB)** | α-synuclein | DAT-SPECT deficit; occipital hypometabolism |
| **Essential tremor (ET)** | None (functional) | DAT-SPECT *normal* — the key distinguishing test |
| **Drug-induced parkinsonism** | Dopamine blockade | DAT-SPECT typically normal |
| **Vascular parkinsonism** | Subcortical strokes | "Lower-body" gait; deep WMH on MRI |

DAT-SPECT's primary clinical use is exactly this: distinguishing PD (and atypical degenerative parkinsonism) from ET, drug-induced, or psychogenic parkinsonism.

## Pathology

PD is defined by intra-neuronal α-synuclein aggregation (Lewy bodies / Lewy neurites) and selective loss of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra pars compacta (SNpc). Striatal dopamine projections degenerate, producing motor symptoms once ~70–80% of striatal dopamine is lost.

[Braak et al., 2003](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0197-4580(02)00065-9) proposed a 6-stage pathological progression starting in the olfactory bulb and dorsal motor vagal nucleus, ascending through brainstem to substantia nigra (stage 3, when motor symptoms emerge) and finally to cortex. The Braak hypothesis remains controversial but motivates the "prodromal PD" research agenda.

## Dopamine PET / SPECT

The molecular-imaging side of PD is genuinely mature.

### DAT-SPECT (DaTscan)

[<sup>123</sup>I]FP-CIT (ioflupane, DaTscan) binds the presynaptic dopamine transporter. SPECT 3 hours post-injection. In PD the putamen is affected first and more severely than caudate, classically with asymmetry matching the clinically-worse side, producing a "comma" → "period" → "absent" pattern.

DAT-SPECT is normal in ET, drug-induced, vascular, psychogenic parkinsonism and abnormal in PD, DLB, MSA, PSP, CBD. It cannot distinguish PD from the atypical degenerative parkinsonisms — that requires MRI features.

### [<sup>18</sup>F]FDOPA PET

Measures dopamine *synthesis* (decarboxylation of FDOPA to fluorodopamine). Reduced striatal uptake in PD. More physiological than DAT-SPECT but PET is less available than SPECT.

### [<sup>11</sup>C]raclopride PET

Measures D2 receptor availability. Used research-wise for dopamine release studies (paired pharmacological challenge) and for distinguishing PD (preserved postsynaptic D2) from MSA-P / PSP (reduced postsynaptic D2).

### [<sup>18</sup>F]AV-133 / VMAT2 PET

Vesicular monoamine transporter 2; another presynaptic marker.

## MR biomarkers of nigrostriatal pathology

The newer story. PD now has a growing menu of MR sequences that show changes in substantia nigra directly, without needing a tracer.

### Neuromelanin-sensitive MRI

Neuromelanin accumulates in dopaminergic SN neurons across the lifespan, then is lost as those neurons die in PD. A T1-weighted gradient-echo sequence with magnetisation transfer at 3T shows SN and locus coeruleus as bright bands. PD shows reduced area and signal contrast.

### QSM and nigral iron

Iron accumulates in the substantia nigra in PD. [Langkammer et al., 2016](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2016.06.047) showed quantitative susceptibility mapping (QSM) reliably distinguishes PD from controls based on increased nigral susceptibility. QSM is now one of the leading candidate MR biomarkers for PD diagnosis and progression.

For the underlying physics of susceptibility imaging, see [Fundamentals → SWI](../fundamentals/sequences/swi.md).

### Nigrosome-1 ("swallow-tail" sign)

On high-resolution SWI at 3T+, healthy SNpc shows a small dorsolateral hyperintense region — nigrosome-1 — sometimes called the "swallow-tail sign". Loss of this sign has high sensitivity and specificity for PD ([Schwarz et al., 2014](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0093814)). It is now in many clinical PD MRI protocols.

### DTI of the nigrostriatal pathway

Reduced FA in SN and nigrostriatal tract in PD, though effect sizes vary and reproducibility across sites has been the limiting factor. Free-water-corrected DTI ([Ofori et al., 2015](https://doi.org/10.1093/brain/awv136)) is a more robust marker showing posterior SN free-water elevation that progresses longitudinally.

### Atypical-parkinsonism MR signs

The classic visual signs:

| Sign | Disease | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Hummingbird / penguin** | PSP | Midbrain atrophy; superior cerebellar peduncle wasting on sagittal T1 |
| **Mickey-Mouse** | PSP | Axial midbrain atrophy |
| **MR Parkinsonism Index** | PSP | Quantitative: (pons × SCP) / (midbrain × MCP) |
| **Hot-cross-bun** | MSA-C | T2-hyperintense cruciform pontine sign |
| **Putaminal slit / rim** | MSA-P | T2-hypointense putaminal rim with hyperintense lateral slit |
| **Asymmetric perirolandic atrophy** | CBD | Cortical atrophy contralateral to worse side |

## Functional imaging of basal-ganglia circuits

Resting-state fMRI of basal-ganglia–thalamo-cortical loops shows altered connectivity in PD — reduced putamen–SMA / putamen–M1 connectivity, increased cortico-striatal–thalamic loop activity. Effect sizes are smaller than molecular biomarkers. PET-based measures of metabolic networks (the **PD-related pattern**, [Eidelberg 2009](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tins.2009.06.003)) — derived from FDG-PET using spatial-covariance analysis — are an older, robust functional biomarker.

## Deep brain stimulation targeting

DBS for advanced PD targets the subthalamic nucleus (STN) or globus pallidus internus (GPi); tremor-dominant patients sometimes target the ventral intermediate nucleus of thalamus (VIM).

Imaging-driven targeting workflow:

1. Preoperative 1.5T or 3T T1, T2, SWI / QSM, sometimes 7T direct visualisation of STN borders.
2. Atlas / template-based target proposal (DISTAL atlas, Ewert / Horn group; basal-ganglia atlases).
3. Microelectrode-recording confirmation intraoperatively.
4. Postoperative CT registered to preoperative MRI to verify lead location.
5. Connectomic / probabilistic stimulation mapping (Lead-DBS, Horn group) to predict optimal contacts.

QSM has become the preferred MR contrast for direct STN visualisation because iron content makes STN crisply hypointense on susceptibility maps.

## Reference datasets

The PD-specific cohorts:

- **PPMI** (Parkinson's Progression Markers Initiative) — the AD-equivalent of ADNI for PD. Multi-site longitudinal MRI, DAT-SPECT, biofluids, clinical, genetics. Includes RBD prodromal cohort and LRRK2 / GBA genetic cohorts. [Marek et al., 2011](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pneurobio.2011.09.005).
- **Parkinson's Progression Markers Initiative 2.0** — refresh with expanded biofluid (α-syn seeding assay) and digital-marker arms.
- **PRePARED / North American PD imaging cohorts** — site-specific cohorts contributing to ENIGMA-PD meta-analysis.
- **ENIGMA-PD** — meta-analytic structural and DTI consortium.
- **Tracking-PD / Oxford PD Discovery cohort** — UK longitudinal cohorts often used for prodromal-marker work.

See [Landmark → Reference datasets](../landmark/datasets.md) for cohort access patterns.

## Open questions

- **Prodromal PD markers.** RBD cohorts have ~6%/year phenoconversion to PD/DLB; imaging that predicts the timing and the final phenotype (PD vs DLB vs MSA) is a major active area. DAT-SPECT, neuromelanin MRI, and α-syn seeding assays are converging here.
- **Disease-modifying biomarkers.** Anti-α-synuclein trials need a quantitative imaging readout that moves on trial timescales (12–24 months). Nigrostriatal DAT decline, free-water in SN, and neuromelanin are all candidates; none has won yet.
- **Subtyping.** Tremor-dominant vs postural-instability-gait-difficulty vs cognitively-vulnerable subtypes have different progression. Imaging-based subtyping with longitudinal validation is unsettled.
- **7T direct visualisation for DBS.** Worth the cost of 7T access? Probably for STN-DBS planning, evidence is consolidating.
- **Differentiating PD from atypical parkinsonism early.** Machine-learning on multimodal MR has gotten close ([Archer et al., 2019](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1474-4422(19)30201-7)) but real-world performance at first visit is still the bottleneck.
- **MR biomarker harmonisation across vendors.** QSM in particular shows site / vendor effects that need correcting before multi-site cohort use.
